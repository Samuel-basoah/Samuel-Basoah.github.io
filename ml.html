Machine Learning & NLP

Quantitative economist and NLP data scientist specializing in turning unstructured economic and financial text into actionable signals. I build end-to-end NLP pipelines in Python and R. I leverage PyTorch and Hugging Face transformers to construct supervisory sentiment indices from large scale regulatory corpora. My work applies fine tuned transformer models (BERT, RoBERTa, FinBERT), machine learning, and econometrics to problems in financial stability, sovereign risk, and banking supervision. I am experienced in SQL based data pipelines, large scale text processing (7.5M+ observations), and communicating model trade-offs clearly to non-technical stakeholders.

Selected Projects

Do Bank Supervisory Performance Signals Matter for Banks?
Ph.D. Research, 2021–Present
  • Built a sentence-level supervisory sentiment index from 1,388 FDIC communications (1978–2025) using fine-tuned FinBERT and RoBERTa transformer models in Python/PyTorch alongside dictionary-based NLP methods.
  • Developed an end-to-end NLP pipeline including API-based data collection, preprocessing, transformer inference, and index construction across 47 years of regulatory text.
  • Applied event-study methods to show bank stock returns respond systematically to FDIC supervisory sentiment, operationalizing unstructured text as a quantitative financial signal.
  • Linked supervisory sentiment to bank-level CAMELS data, finding associations with stronger deposit growth, improved asset quality, and reduced bank failure risk.
Sovereign Default Risk & Digital Adoption
Ph.D. Research, 2021–Present
  • Used NLP methods to identify digital fiscal reform events across 46 emerging economies from policy and news text, combined with World Bank DAI and EMBI spread data.
  • Built and calibrated a quantitative sovereign default model; simulated 25,000 paths to generate impulse responses, reproduce empirical moments, and produce model-simulated datasets.
  • Documented that digital fiscal reforms are associated with improvements in tax revenue, translating NLP-identified reforms into macro-financial signals.
Predicting Bank Failures Using Text-Based Regulatory Indices
Ph.D. Research, 2024–Present
  • Developing machine learning models to predict bank failures in the United States using text-derived regulatory sentiment indices combined with structured financial data.
