Teaching Philosophy

My teaching philosophy is grounded in the belief that effective education empowers and transforms students, equipping them not only with knowledge but with the critical tools to solve real-world problems. I have served as a Teaching Assistant for 13 distinct courses across 24 terms, and play an active role in my department’s study nights, workshops, and mentoring programs for students and new Teaching Assistants.

Since Fall 2022, I have served as a Head Teaching Assistant responsible for supporting and mentoring fellow TAs, and since 2024 I lead and co-organize the Economics Department’s workshops for incoming Teaching Assistants. I have mentored 15 Teaching Assistants and consistently rank among the top five in the department’s TA evaluations.

In Spring 2026, I was nominated by SFU’s Centre for Educational Excellence to present on the fundamentals of teaching for first-time Teaching Assistants — how to run tutorials confidently, fairly, and professionally. My teaching interests include macroeconomics, international finance and trade, intermediate microeconomics, money and banking, public economics, econometrics, and environmental economics.

Awards: Terry Heaps Outstanding Teaching Assistant Award (2024) · Faculty of Arts and Social Sciences Best Teaching Assistant Award, Simon Fraser University (2025)
